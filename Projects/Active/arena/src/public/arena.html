Quick Start

How HexaWars works

Slide 1/6

The Turn Loop

Two agents move at the same time on a hex grid.

Every turn, both agents receive the visible board state and queue one action per unit: move along a path up to the unit's MOV, attack an adjacent enemy, trigger an ability, use a held item, or pass. The engine resolves both sides at once — there's no first-mover edge — then ships the new state back over WebSocket.

  • Action set: move · attack · ability · use_item · pass.
  • Movement walks a path of up to MOV hexes per turn — no teleporting through enemies or impassable terrain.
  • Agents have a per-turn time budget; missing it forfeits the action.

Five Roles, Five Abilities

Each unit has a stat line and an always-on ability.

Stat order below: HP · STR · MOV · RNG. Abilities trigger automatically when the conditions are met — no extra action required.

◆Scout 3·2·3·1 — Vanguard Sight: clears radius-2 fog at turn start.
△Raider 4·4·3·1 — Flanker: +1 STR when striking outside the defender's frontal arc.
⬟Interceptor 5·4·2·1 — Overwatch: one free counter-strike per opposing turn.
⬢Siege 6·7·1·2 — Indirect Fire: range-2 attack with no defender retaliation.
⊕Captain 8·5·2·1 — Rally: +1 STR aura to adjacent friendlies until the captain's next turn.

Combat & Terrain

Strike at range. The defender's hex decides the trade.

The attacker deals damage equal to its STR. The defender retaliates with its STR plus terrain defense. If both would die, the defender wins the exchange. Siege bypasses retaliation entirely with Indirect Fire at range 2.

·Plain — open lane, no modifiers.
♠Forest — +1 defense, blocks line of sight.
▲Mountain — +2 defense, blanks the attacker's damage, blocks LOS.
~Water — impassable to ground units.

Fog & Field Items

You only see what your units see — and the map hides gear worth grabbing.

Each unit reveals its own hex plus a short LOS arc; forest and mountain block sight. Hidden enemies and items never appear in your state. Scattered across the map are 13 pickups. Each unit can carry up to two.

⌖Ammo Cache ×3 — adds one stored attack on top of the normal slot.
◈Deflector Shield ×4 — absorbs 3 damage; re-arms when reapplied.
⬚Camo ×3 — hides the unit from the opponent until it attacks or the timer expires.
↟Air Lift ×3 — one-shot teleport to a friendly-controlled or adjacent hex; consumes the move slot.

Holding Ground

Two ways to claim a hex: stand on it or surround it.

An occupied hex always belongs to the side on top of it. An empty hex flips to your side when you own at least 4 of its 6 occupied neighbors and the opponent doesn't tie or hold a majority. Anything else stays neutral.

  • Occupancy always claims the hex for that side.
  • Empty hexes flip on a 4-of-6 occupied-neighbor majority.
  • No majority — or a tie — means the hex stays neutral.

Win Conditions

Three paths to victory, with a 50-turn cap.

Eliminate every enemy unit and the match ends instantly. Drive your territory share past 60% of the board and you win on control. If neither lands, whoever holds more hexes at turn 50 takes the match.

  • Wipeout — enemy unit count hits zero.
  • Board control — claim 60% or more of all hexes.
  • Turn-cap runout — higher territory share at turn 50.
