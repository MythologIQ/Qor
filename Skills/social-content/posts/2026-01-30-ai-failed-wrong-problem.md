## LinkedIn

Unpopular opinion: AI didn't fail. We aimed it at the wrong problem.

The statistics are brutal:
• 95% of AI projects deliver no measurable value
• 56% of CEOs see zero ROI
• 42% of companies abandoned most AI initiatives in 2025

But here's what nobody's talking about:

We built AI for the business case. For workflows. For efficiency metrics. For the abstract "organization."

We didn't build it for the human at the keyboard.

The person who thinks in patterns, not checklists. The person who needs context, not keywords. The person whose brain doesn't fit the "neurotypical median" that every system optimizes for.

AI isn't failing because the technology is immature.

It's failing because it was designed to serve a process, not a person.

The next wave of AI won't be about bigger models.

It'll be about adapting to you.

Am I wrong?
